While the Amazon Firestick is incredibly popular, Android TV Boxes offer a more open and customizable platform for IPTV streaming. Because they run a full version of Android, you often have more flexibility and access to a wider range of apps.

This guide will cover the two primary methods for installing any IPTV service on your Android TV Box.

## What is an Android TV Box? {#what-is-an-android-tv-box}

An "Android TV Box" can refer to two things:
1.  **Official Android TV:** Devices like the Nvidia Shield or Chromecast with Google TV, which run the official "Android TV" OS.
2.  **Generic Android Box:** Cheaper, unbranded boxes that run a "stock" version of Android, similar to a tablet.

The good news is that both types use the same installation methods.

## Before You Start: Enable 'Unknown Sources' {#before-you-start}

Just like on a Firestick, you must first give your device permission to install apps from outside the Google Play Store.

1.  From your home screen, go to **Settings**.
2.  Navigate to **Security & Restrictions** (on some devices, this may be under "Advanced Settings" or "Device Preferences").
3.  Find the **"Unknown sources"** option.
4.  Toggle it to **"On"** or "Allow." You may need to do this specifically for the "Downloader" app, which we'll install next.

## Method 1: Installing via the Google Play Store (Easiest) {#method-1-google-play-store}

If your IPTV provider has an app on the Google Play Store (or if you're using a popular player like TiviMate), this is the simplest way:

1.  Open the **Google Play Store** from your home screen.
2.  Search for your provider's app name.
3.  Click **"Install"** and then **"Open"**.
4.  Log in with your username, password, and server URL.

## Method 2: Sideloading with the 'Downloader' App (Most Common) {#method-2-sideloading}

Most premium IPTV services require you to "sideload" a specific .apk file. The process is very similar to the one used for the [Amazon Firestick](/blog/the-ultimate-2025-guide-to-installing-iptv-on-amazon-firestick).

### Step 1: Install the 'Downloader' App {#step-1-install-downloader}

If your box has the Google Play Store, search for **"Downloader by AFTVnews"** and install it. If your box doesn't have the Play Store, you may need to open your built-in web browser and search for the Downloader app's .apk file to install it first.

### Step 2: Enter Your IPTV App's .apk Link {#step-2-enter-apk-link}

When you sign up for your IPTV service, you'll get an .apk download link.

1.  Open the **Downloader** app.
2.  In the main URL bar, type in the **exact .apk link** provided by your IPTV provider.
3.  Click **"Go"**.

### Step 3: Install and Log In {#step-3-install-and-log-in}

The app file will download.

1.  Once the download is complete, an installation window will appear. Click **"Install"**.
2.  After it's installed, click **"Open"**.
3.  The app will launch. Enter your IPTV subscription credentials (username, password, and server URL) to start streaming.
